# A friendlier website + 4 easy ways to get paid

Hi — this is a free concept redesign of the Parkway Auto Spa website, put
together after a visit where a customer couldn't pay because the shop's
internet was down. That moment is the whole reason for this: **right now, if the
connection drops, you can't take money.** That's a single point of failure, and
it costs you sales.

This demo shows how to fix that — and gives the site a modern refresh while
we're at it. Nothing here charges any real cards; it's a working preview you can
click through.

---

## What's new

**A cleaner, mobile-first website** that still feels like you — same blue, same
"treat your car to a spa day," same #1-in-McKinney pride — just modernized.

**Four ways to get paid, most of which don't rely on the shop's Wi-Fi:**

1. **Pay by phone + QR code.** Put a small QR sign at the register. The customer
   scans it with their phone camera and pays over *their own* cellular data. If
   the shop internet is down, the sale still goes through.
2. **Book online with a deposit.** Customers reserve a wash or detail and leave
   a small deposit — fewer no-shows, money in before they arrive.
3. **Monthly memberships.** Sign people up online for unlimited-wash plans that
   bill automatically every month — steady, recurring income.
4. **Gift cards & prepaid bundles.** Sell gift cards and wash packages online —
   cash in the door before the first wash.

## See it for yourself

1. Open the folder you were sent.
2. Double-click **`index.html`** — it opens in any web browser.
3. Click around. Try the **Pay** page (scan the QR with your phone), **Book**,
   **Membership**, and **Gift cards**.

When a checkout asks for a card, it's a safe demo — use the test number
`4242 4242 4242 4242`, any future expiry, any 3-digit code. **No real money
moves.** Every checkout clearly says "Demo — no real charge."

---

## What it takes to go live

The website itself can go live today (free hosting options exist). To turn the
demo checkouts into *real* payments, you just need a payment account:

- **Option A — Stripe** (stripe.com): create an account, get your keys.
- **Option B — Square** (squareup.com): if you already use Square in the shop,
  this keeps everything in one place.

Either one connects at a single, clearly-marked spot in the code
(`js/checkout.js`, the "GO LIVE SEAM"). A web developer can wire it up in a
short sitting. Typical processing fees are about 2.6–2.9% + 30¢ per
transaction — only paid when you actually get paid.

After that you can add real online booking calendars and automatic text/email
receipts whenever you're ready.
